Visual token pruning — keep-ratio sweep

LLaVA-1.5-7B, CPU-only (Apple M4 Pro), build-prune @ 82f2ccb5c · keep ∈ {1.0, 0.75, 0.5, 0.25, 0.1, 0.05} · n=5 + warmup, cooled 30s/run

TTFT_llm speedup climbs from 1.0× to 5.25× at keep=0.05, realizing 88–100% of the theoretical ceiling from token-count-linear prefill scaling (H1) across every ratio tested — strong support for the CPU-linear-prefill hypothesis. TTFT_vlm never turns around within the tested range: no break-even where pruning overhead exceeds savings was observed (H2) down to keep=0.05. The vision encoder's share of total time triples (12% → 39%) as prefill shrinks, since all 576 patches still cross all 23 ViT layers regardless of keep ratio — pruning only shrinks the LLM side.

Qualitative degradation at the most aggressive ratio. Generated text is byte-identical across keep ∈ {1.0, 0.75, 0.5, 0.25} ("two cats lying on a pink couch…"). At keep=0.1 the description already drifts (leads with "a couch", drops "sleeping peacefully"). At keep=0.05 (29 image tokens) the model hallucinates — "a couch with two remote controls" in place of the two cats. One image, temp=0, not a systematic eval — but a real, unprompted failure mode worth flagging rather than only reporting the speedup curve.

Theoretical ceiling (linear in token count)
Theoretical ceiling = TTFT_llm(keep=1.0) × (n_tokens(1.0) / n_tokens(r)), i.e. the speedup if prefill scaled perfectly linearly with token count. Measured tracks it closely throughout (88–100% realized) — the gap grows slowly as keep shrinks, consistent with a small fixed per-call overhead that matters more as the variable part shrinks.
