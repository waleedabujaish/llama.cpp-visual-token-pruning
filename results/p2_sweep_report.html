Visual token pruning — keep-ratio sweep

LLaVA-1.5-7B, CPU-only (Apple M4 Pro), build-prune @ 82f2ccb5c · keep ∈ {1.0, 0.75, 0.5, 0.25, 0.1, 0.05, 0.03, 0.02, 0.015, 0.01} · n=5 + warmup, cooled 30s/run

TTFT_llm speedup climbs to 5.25× at keep=0.05, realizing 88–100% of the theoretical ceiling from token-count-linear prefill scaling (H1) — strong support for the CPU-linear-prefill hypothesis, holding across the whole original range. Extending the sweep down to keep=0.01 answers H2: TTFT_vlm does turn around. Its minimum (mean) is at keep=0.05 (1690ms); every ratio below that is higher, not lower — keep=0.03 jumps to 2566ms (+52%). But at this extreme end (K=6–17 tokens) run-to-run noise explodes (encode_ms std goes from ±1–6ms in the clean range to ±65–117ms here, with zero thermal-drift signature — load average stayed flat) and best-case individual runs in several of the new cells undercut keep=0.05's mean entirely. Read as: a real turnaround, but one whose exact location and shape isn't resolved at n=5 in this regime — see the chart and table below for the honest picture, not a single clean number.

Qualitative degradation, escalating with keep ratio — and this is exactly why the extended range (keep ≤ 0.05) is a scientific probe for H2, not a candidate operating point. Generated text is byte-identical across keep ∈ {1.0, 0.75, 0.5, 0.25}. At keep=0.1 it drifts (same cats, softer phrasing). At keep=0.05 (29 tokens) the model already hallucinates — "two remote controls" for two cats — before the extension even begins. Below that it gets stranger, not better: keep=0.03 elaborates the hallucination ("a TV remote and a video game remote"); keep=0.02 produces a hybrid ("two cats… each with a remote control in their paws"); keep=0.015 drops the cats entirely again; keep=0.01 (6 tokens) breaks completely — "two photographs… a black and white image of a man standing in a field", content with no relation to the input at all. One image, temp=0, not a systematic eval — but do not read a compute break-even found at keep=0.01–0.03 as "so prune to 1–3%": the model's outputs are already unreliable well above that range.
